Titles of the Textbooks!

BRVC Teens

We have created titles and a brand for the textbooks in the BRVC educational program. These textbooks are distinctive in that they are interconnected and build on one another, making it possible to work with children of all ages from preschool to adolescence for a period of ten years. The titles we have selected illustrate the gradual influence of educators on students to enhance the quality of their lives and develop their abilities. The series' logo is a hot air balloon, which has three meanings: it represents how educators rise above societal problems and issues that students face, how students become closer to God, to make the hot air balloon fly, it is always necessary to maintain the correct air temperature, which means that working with students should be consistent and not limited to one meeting, as children need constant educator influence. The entire series is called Progressio, meaning gradual development in Latin, as each prior step is linked to the next. The first textbook, aimed at 3-4-year-olds, is called "Fasten your seatbelts," as the preparation for success is crucial even though these students are too young for flight. The second textbook, for 5-6-year-olds, is titled "Gain altitude" where students gradually move upward and the focus is on developing a love for learning. The third textbook, for 7-8-year-olds, is "Move up" as students begin school and require encouragement and support. The fourth textbook, for 9-10-year-olds, is "Set the direction" recognizing the importance of moral orientation at this age. The fifth textbook, for 11-12-year-olds, is "Maintain balance" as students need to remain on the path they have chosen, avoiding any adolescent crises. The final textbook, for 13-14-year-olds, is "Open horizons" as students with acquired knowledge move into adulthood and must apply it.
